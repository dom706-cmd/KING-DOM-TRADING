1. What KingDom Is

KingDom is a scanner and monitor system. It is designed to find potential setups, separate trade-ready ideas from watch / monitor ideas, and explain why names passed or failed. It is not a black box and it should not be treated like one.

Core principle: if no strict trade-ready names pass, KingDom should still surface the best monitor/watch seeds instead of looking empty or broken.

2. Mental Model

Trade-ready

  • Best strict setups right now
  • Passed the tighter gating stack
  • Most actionable if you are looking for immediate execution

Monitor / Watch

  • Interesting names that did not fully pass strict entry conditions
  • Still worth watching for improvement
  • Useful for paper trading and workflow validation

3. Main Workflow

  1. Choose a quick preset or load a saved preset.
  2. Run a scan.
  3. Read the top section carefully:
    • Mode
    • Trade-ready count
    • Rejected count
    • Seed pool
    • Primary message
  4. If trade-ready is zero, review the monitor/watch fallback names rather than assuming the scan failed.
  5. Open candidate details and read the why this ranked here section before acting.
  6. Move interesting names into a monitoring workflow.

KingDom supports multiple scan strategies selectable per run. Each has a distinct edge and time-of-day profile:

News Enrichment (all strategies)

Every scan result now includes news_headline and news_age_hours sourced from the Alpaca News feed. A candidate moving 5× ATR with a 2-hour-old headline is a very different trade from one with no news. If a candidate shows — for news, it's a pure technical move with no identifiable catalyst.

Institutional Footprint Score (parabolic + gap_and_go)

Every Alpaca bar includes a trade count (n field). KingDom computes avg_trade_size = volume / trade_count per session and compares it to the 20-day historical baseline. A stock with 10M shares in 50 prints is institutional accumulation. The same 10M shares in 200,000 prints is retail FOMO. The Inst +X% badge in candidate notes reflects the multiplier applied to the composite score. No commercial scanner does this calculation.

Opening Auction Confirmation (parabolic + gap_and_go, post-9:30am)

After the market opens, KingDom fetches the opening auction print via Alpaca’s /v2/stocks/auctions endpoint and computes auction_gap_pct = (auction_price - pm_last) / pm_last × 100. A positive Auction +X% badge (green) means the stock opened above its PM price — institutional MOO buyers were present and the move is confirmed. A negative badge (red) means sellers absorbed at open — likely fade, not a breakout. This signal addresses the AKAN problem directly: knowing the opening auction direction before you commit to chasing.

Analyze Watchlist — Setup Quality Indicators (Gap Orders card)

When you click ⚖ Analyze Watchlist in the Gap Orders card, each symbol in your Desk Watchlist is scored against its previous session’s data. In addition to the plan status (Intact / Near Entry / Triggered / Invalidated), the table shows four Setup Quality columns that tell you how clean the setup was at EOD:

ColumnGreen ✓Amber ~Red ✗What it tells you
Close% ≥ 60%30–60%< 30% Where yesterday’s close landed in the day’s range (0% = LOD, 100% = HOD). ≥ 60% means it closed near the top of the range — sellers never regained control. < 30% means it gave back most of the move before close — weak setup.
RVOL ≥ 1.5×0.8–1.5×< 0.8× Yesterday’s volume vs the 20-day average (computed from 60 days of daily history for accuracy). Elevated volume on the setup day confirms conviction. Low RVOL = thin, uncommitted move.
News < 12h old12–48hNo news Age of the most recent headline from Alpaca News. Hover the cell to read the full headline. A fresh catalyst (< 12h) means the story is still active going into the next session. No news = pure technical move.
Ext% 3–30%0–3%> 30% Yesterday’s day move (open→close). 3–30% is the sweet spot — meaningful move with room left to work. > 30% = extended, hard to get a clean entry without chasing. < 3% = barely moved, low conviction.

Use these four columns together with the plan status to prioritize. An Intact name with all four green — strong HOD close, elevated RVOL, fresh catalyst, not overextended — is your highest-quality setup. An Intact name with red Close% and red RVOL is technically valid but structurally weak; treat it with lower conviction.

6. Alpaca Data APIs — What’s Wired In

KingDom uses three Alpaca data endpoints automatically. You do not need to configure anything — they run on your existing API key.

APIUsed ByWhat It Does
Market Movers
/v1beta1/screener/stocks/movers		 gap_and_go When you run gap_and_go with no symbol list, it auto-seeds from the top 25 real-time gainers + 25 losers (SIP data vs prev close). These are the actual gap candidates at open.
Most Actives
/v1beta1/screener/stocks/most-actives		 atr_expansion When you run atr_expansion with no symbol list, it auto-seeds from the top 100 most-active stocks by volume. You never need to pass a symbol list for these strategies.
Snapshots
/v2/stocks/snapshots		 eod_momentum Pre-filter pass before fetching 3-month daily bars. One API call per 100 symbols returns today’s OHLCV + yesterday’s close — symbols that clearly didn’t move are discarded immediately, cutting API calls by 80–90% on large universes like saturday_prep.
News
/v1beta1/news		 gap_and_go, atr_expansion, eod_momentum After candidates are built, the most recent headline per symbol is fetched and attached as news_headline + news_age_hours. A "—" means no news in the last 48 hours — pure technical move.
Bar Trade Count
n field in every bar		 parabolic, gap_and_go Already included in every bar response — zero extra API calls. KingDom extracts the Trades column and computes avg trade size vs 20-day baseline. Drives the Institutional Footprint Score multiplier shown as Inst +X% on candidates.
Auctions
/v2/stocks/auctions		 parabolic, gap_and_go Fetched once per session at 9:30am after the opening auction clears. Returns the opening print price and volume. Attached to candidates as auction_gap_pct — positive = confirmed gap up beyond PM price, negative = faded at open.

Note on the Snapshot pre-filter: it only filters on the day it’s run. On weekends (saturday_prep), the snapshot date won’t match the session date (Friday), so the pre-filter automatically bypasses itself and the full daily bar fetch runs as normal. You always get correct results — the pre-filter is a speed optimization, not a correctness change.

7. Understanding Results

If you see trade-ready names

  • These are the strictest actionable outputs.
  • Still review individual setup logic and context before acting.

If you see monitor / watch fallback

  • This means the scan found useful names, but none fully passed the trade-ready gate.
  • This is not a broken scan.
  • Use these names for watchlisting, paper trading, and intraday reassessment.

Zero-result diagnostics

When no strict candidates pass, KingDom should explain why. Typical reasons include:

  • filtered price
  • avg20 dollar volume too low
  • confidence too low
  • ORB retest ML too low
  • chase too high

8. Candidate Detail Panel

Use the candidate detail view to answer:

  • Why did this symbol rank here?
  • Was it trade-ready or just a watch seed?
  • What are the plan levels: entry, stop, targets?
  • What catalyst / news context exists? (check news_headline + age)
  • What score breakdown drove the ranking?

9. Alert Tape and Monitor States

KingDom’s monitor should distinguish between:

  • Near trigger – setup approaching action point
  • Ready – conditions becoming actionable
  • Triggered – conditions fired
  • Suppressed – conditions intentionally muted / degraded

Use alert cooldown and alert level controls to avoid noise.

10. Paper Trading Workflow

Paper trading here means taking notes, not sending orders.

Record for each paper trade

  • symbol
  • time
  • side
  • entry
  • stop
  • target(s)
  • trade-ready or watch fallback?
  • why selected?
  • why entered?
  • why held / trimmed / stopped?
  • what did KingDom get right or wrong?

11. Daily Operating Routine

  1. Night before / weekend: run saturday_prep → review EOD momentum candidates → save best to Desk Watchlist with entry/stop/side.
  2. Pre-market: Analyze Watchlist (Gap Orders card) → use the four Setup Quality columns (Close%, RVOL, News, Ext%) to prioritize Intact names — all-green = highest conviction → trim invalidated names → Generate Orders for survivors.
  3. Pre-market (4am–9:29am): run parabolic_watch — auto-seeds from Market Movers gainers. Look for candidates with high acceleration_ratio (still moving at 7–8am), strong Inst factor (institutional prints), and fresh catalyst. Add the best names to your Desk Watchlist before 9:30.
  4. At open: run gap_and_go (auto-seeds from Market Movers) and/or atr_expansion (auto-seeds from Most Actives) with no symbol list required. Check the Auction badge on each candidate — green = confirmed gap up beyond PM, red = fading at open.
  5. Review trade-ready names first. If none, review watch fallback names.
  6. Check news_headline on every candidate — know why it’s moving before you act.
  7. Open candidate details on the best 3–10 names.
  8. Pick paper trades with explicit reasoning.
  9. Review outcomes later and calibrate thresholds/ranking.

12. What KingDom Still Is Not

  • Not an automatic money printer
  • Not a substitute for judgment
  • Not finished forever
  • Not better than every incumbent tool in every dimension yet

13. What to Trust Most

  1. Clear ranking logic
  2. Diagnostics explaining why names passed or failed
  3. Monitor/watch fallback when strict trade-ready is empty
  4. Repeated paper-trade validation over time
